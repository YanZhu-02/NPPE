Data and Codes from the paper “Local Spectral-Entropy Perturbability Reveals Hidden Spreading Influence in Complex Networks”

The data and custom codes supporting this study are available in the GitHub repository provided in the manuscript. The experiments are conducted on sixteen undirected networks, including four empirical networks and twelve synthetic benchmark networks. The empirical networks are Facebook-like messages (FB), Email (EM), Wiki-Vote (WV), and Lastfm-Asia (FM). The synthetic networks include three Barabási–Albert (BA) networks, three LFR benchmark networks, three Erdős–Rényi (ER) networks, and three Watts–Strogatz (WS) networks. The data and custom code are reported as available at the GitHub repository of the project.

The 4 empirical network data come from the following references: Facebook-like messages (FB): Rossi, R.A., Ahmed, N.K., 2014. NetworkRepository: An interactive data repository with multi-scale visual analytics. arXiv:1410.3560. Email (EM): Guimerà, R., Danon, L., Díaz-Guilera, A., Giralt, F., Arenas, A., 2003. Self-similar community structure in a network of human interactions. Physical Review E 68, 065103. Wiki-Vote (WV): Rossi, R.A., Ahmed, N.K., 2015. The network data repository with interactive graph analytics and visualization. Proceedings of the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence. Lastfm-Asia (FM): Rozemberczki, B., Sarkar, R., 2020. Characteristic functions on graphs: Birds of a feather, from statistical descriptors to parametric models. Proceedings of the 29th ACM International Conference on Information & Knowledge Management, pp. 1325–1334.

The 12 synthetic networks are generated from four standard network models. For the BA networks, each newly added node is connected to 5, 4, and 3 existing nodes in BA1, BA2, and BA3, respectively. For the LFR benchmark networks, the degree exponents are 2.3, 2.5, and 2.7; the community-size exponents are 1.5, 1.8, and 2.0; and the mixing parameters are 0.05, 0.10, and 0.15 for LFR1, LFR2, and LFR3, respectively. For the ER networks, the connection probabilities are 0.00331, 0.01, and 0.02. For the WS networks, each node is initially connected to its six nearest neighbours on a ring lattice, and the rewiring probabilities are 0.01, 0.05, and 0.09 for WS1, WS2, and WS3, respectively. The corresponding model references are Barabási and Albert for BA networks, Lancichinetti–Fortunato–Radicchi for LFR benchmarks, Erdős and Rényi for ER random graphs, and Watts and Strogatz for small-world networks.
